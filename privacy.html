Project SensorWeb Privacy Notice

Last Updated: June 30, 2016

When Mozilla (that’s us), receives information from you, our Mozilla Privacy Policy describes how we handle that information. You must be over the age of 13 to participate in Project SensorWeb.

Things you should know

You send Mozilla data when you set up a sensor. This includes your sensor ID and sensor results. We also receive your location, name, email address, category of interest, user ID, and picture when you send us these to indicate your interest to participate in Project SensorWeb.

  • We will receive your sensor ID and sensor results such as air pollution levels once every minute.
  • If you do not want to report your sensor ID and sensor results to us, you should turn off the power supply to your sensor.
  • We would expect the location you sent to be the place of your sensor, but cannot tell if it is not.
  • If you wish to change your location, you will need to obtain a new sensor ID from us for the new location as your existing sensor ID is associated with your original location.
  • We will display your chosen user ID, picture, and location and results of your sensor on the Project SensorWeb web site.
  • We will also use your name, email address, and category of interest to notify you via email about developments with Project SensorWeb that may interest you such as inviting you to participate, if you are not already. You can opt-out from email communications replying to email we sent with the message “Unsubscribe me.”

We connect with Project SensorWeb participants using email and on the Project SensorWeb website. To better understand user engagement with Project SensorWeb, we also use cookies, analytics, and non-Mozilla services.

  • Your interaction with the Project SensorWeb website is collected as part of a standard server log. This server log data will be associated with your IP address and user ID, and used for troubleshooting.
  • We use cookies, clear GIFs, and analytics in our communications and on our webpages. For example, analytics are used to measure engagement with our emails and webpages. Learn more about our privacy practices in connection with websites, communications, and cookies here.
  • We collect information about the source that referred you to Project SensorWeb (e.g., email, webpage link, etc.).
  • We use services of other companies like Google, for emails, analytics, and surveys. We encourage you to review their privacy policies and terms, as they are different from ours.

We may need to change this notice. The updates will be posted online.

  • If the changes are substantive, we will announce the update through Mozilla’s usual channels for such announcements such as blog posts and forums. Your continued use of Project SensorWeb after the effective date of such changes constitutes your acceptance of such changes.
